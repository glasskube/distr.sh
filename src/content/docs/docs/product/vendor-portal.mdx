The Vendor Portal serves as the control plane for vendors, providing access to all Distr features, including [application onboarding](/docs/guides/onboarding-a-helm-app/), [artifact registry](/docs/guides/container-registry/) management, and monitoring of end-customer deployment states. In contrast, the [Customer Portal](/docs/product/customer-portal/) offers a simplified interface for end customers, allowing them to configure and manage their deployments while centralizing release information and helpful instructions for operating the vendorâ€™s software.

## Key Features

1. **Tutorials**: Start with the [Quickstart](/docs/quickstart/#getting-started-with-platform-tutorials) to get familiar with the platform.
2. **Dashboard**: View health status for agents and artifacts across all deployments.
3. **Agents**: Use the [Agents](/docs/product/agents/) section to add applications, configure deployments monitor the health and view deployment logs.
4. **Registry**: Browse and view consumption metrics of your [artifacts](/docs/product/registry/) in the _Registry_ section.
5. **Licenses**: Define license rules to control access to specific artifact and application versions or tags.
6. **Customers**: Invite [customers](/docs/guides/onboarding-a-new-customer/) to your organization and update their profile details.
7. **Users**: Add and manage internal team members to your Distr account.
8. **Branding**: [White-label](/docs/product/branding/) the customer portal with your branding and provide customers with self-service instructions and documentation.
9. **Settings**: Set your organization name, configure your registry [slug](/docs/guides/container-registry/#1-set-your-registry-slug) in the _Settings_ section.
   If a custom domain is configured, you can also see the configured domain name here.
10. **Account button**: Use the account section to create [Personal Access Tokens](/docs/integrations/personal-access-token/) associated to your account for API, SKD and Registry authentication.
